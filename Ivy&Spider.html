Ivy and Spider Plant Care

Taking care of life on earth - right in your dorm room!

1) Location

Even more so than other plants, ivy must be kept in very bright light. If the conditions allow, consider moving the plant slightly outdoors on some days during the summer months. During other times of the year, a very well lit windowsill will do the trick

2) Water

Ivy plants prefer their soil to be a little bit on the drier side. Water about 16oz once every two weeks. If you notice the soil is too dry and crumbly, add 8oz of water. In drier times of the year (think winter), you may have to water once every week. Always drain excess water that collects in the bottom dish of the plant.

3) Repotting

If things are going well, the ivy will likely outgrow its pot within 6 months. At that point, repot the plant in a pot about twice the size of the original.
